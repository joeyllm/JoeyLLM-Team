# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

JoeyLLM contributors are expected to treat each other with respect, professionalism, and patience.

We want this repository to be a collaborative environment where people can ask questions, share ideas, review work, and learn without being dismissed or talked over.

## Expected Behaviour

Contributors should:

- communicate respectfully and assume good intent
- give feedback that is specific, constructive, and relevant to the work
- be open to questions, corrections, and different levels of experience
- keep discussions focused on improving the project
- credit other people’s work and contributions appropriately

## Unacceptable Behaviour

The following behaviour is not acceptable:

- personal attacks, insults, or harassment
- discriminatory or exclusionary language
- hostile, intimidating, or deliberately disruptive behaviour
- repeated bad-faith arguments or dismissive review comments
- sharing private or sensitive information without permission

## Collaboration Expectations

- Disagree with ideas, not people
- Explain technical decisions clearly
- If a discussion becomes blocked, move to evidence, examples, or a tutor/team lead decision
- Respect the project’s documentation and workflow conventions

## Reporting Issues

If someone’s behaviour is making collaboration difficult or unsafe, raise it with the team lead, tutor, or the appropriate course staff member.

When reporting an issue, include:

- what happened
- where it happened
- any relevant screenshots or links
- what support or outcome you are requesting

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to repository discussions, pull requests, issues, reviews, shared project communication, and related collaboration spaces for JoeyLLM.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers or teaching staff may remove comments, ask for behaviour changes, or restrict participation if this code of conduct is not followed.
